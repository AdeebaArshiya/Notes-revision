Leg Workout

Warm-Up for leg Exercises

Before jumping into Leg workouts, it’s important to warm up your body. A good warm-up increases blood flow, loosens your muscles, and gets your core ready to work harder while reducing the risk of injury. Start with 3–5 minutes of light cardio, like jogging in place or jumping jacks, to raise your heart rate. Then, add some dynamic stretches such as torso twists, standing side bends, or high knees to activate your core. This short routine will prepare your abs and make your workout more effective.

Chest Exercises

  1. Squats – A powerful move that strengthens thighs, glutes, and core while improving balance.
    Do 3 sets of 12–15 reps.



  2. Side-Lying Leg Lift – Targets the outer thighs and hips, helping tone and stabilize the lower body.
    Do 3 sets of 12–15 reps per side.



  3. Lunges – Builds strength in legs and glutes while improving balance and coordination.
    Do 3 sets of 10–12 reps per leg.



  4. Donkey Kick – Focuses on glutes and hamstrings for better lower-body strength.
    Do 3 sets of 12–15 reps per leg.



  5. Knee-to-Chest Stretch – A relaxing stretch that releases tension in the lower back and hips.
    Hold 15–20 seconds per leg, repeat 2–3 times.



  6. Wall Calf Raise – Strengthens calf muscles and improves ankle stability.
    Do 3 sets of 15–20 reps.
