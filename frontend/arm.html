Arm Workout

Warm-Up for ArmExercises

Before jumping into arm workouts, it’s important to warm up your body. A good warm-up increases blood flow, loosens your muscles, and gets your core ready to work harder while reducing the risk of injury. Start with 3–5 minutes of light cardio, like jogging in place or jumping jacks, to raise your heart rate. Then, add some dynamic stretches such as torso twists, standing side bends, or high knees to activate your core. This short routine will prepare your abs and make your workout more effective.

Arm Exercise

  1. Arm Raise – Strengthens shoulders and upper arms while improving posture.
    Do 3 sets of 12–15 reps.



  2. Tricep Dips – Targets the back of your arms to tone and build tricep strength.
    Do 3 sets of 10–12 reps.



  3. Arm Circles – A great warm-up move that loosens shoulders and increases blood flow.
    Do 3 sets of 15–20 circles forward and backward.



  4. Mountain Climbers – A cardio-core workout that burns calories while strengthening abs, arms, and legs.

    3 sets of 30–40 seconds



  5. Inchworms – Works arms, chest, and core while improving flexibility.
    Do 3 sets of 8–10 reps



  6. Triceps Stretch – A cool-down stretch that relieves tension and improves flexibility in the arms.
    Hold 15–20 seconds per arm, repeat 2–3 times
