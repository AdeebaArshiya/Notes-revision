Abs Workout

Warm-Up for Abs Exercises

Before jumping into ab workouts, it’s important to warm up your body. A good warm-up increases blood flow, loosens your muscles, and gets your core ready to work harder while reducing the risk of injury. Start with 3–5 minutes of light cardio, like jogging in place or jumping jacks, to raise your heart rate. Then, add some dynamic stretches such as torso twists, standing side bends, or high knees to activate your core. This short routine will prepare your abs and make your workout more effective.

Abs Beginner

  1. Jumping Jacks – A full-body cardio move that raises your heart rate, warms up your muscles, and boosts stamina

    3 sets of 30–40 seconds



  2. Abdominal Crunches – A classic core exercise that strengthens your upper abs and builds a toned midsection

    3 sets of 15–20 reps



  3. Russian Twist – A rotational core move that works your obliques and improves balance.

    3 sets of 20 reps (10 each side)



  4. Mountain Climbers – A cardio-core workout that burns calories while strengthening abs, arms, and legs.

    3 sets of 30–40 seconds



  5. Leg Raise – Great for lower abs, this exercise helps build strength and improve core stability.

    3 sets of 12–15 reps



  6. Heel Touch – A simple yet effective move that targets your oblique muscles for better side definition.

    3 sets of 20 reps (10 each side)



  7. Plank – A stability exercise that engages your entire core, improving posture and endurance.

    3 sets of 20–40 seconds hold
