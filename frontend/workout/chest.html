Warm-Up for Chest Exercises

Before jumping into Chest workouts, it’s important to warm up your body. A good warm-up increases blood flow, loosens your muscles, and gets your core ready to work harder while reducing the risk of injury. Start with 3–5 minutes of light cardio, like jogging in place or jumping jacks, to raise your heart rate. Then, add some dynamic stretches such as torso twists, standing side bends, or high knees to activate your core. This short routine will prepare your abs and make your workout more effective.

Chest Exercises

  1. Jumping Jacks – A full-body cardio move that raises your heart rate, warms up your muscles, and boosts stamina

    3 sets of 30–40 seconds



  2. Incline Push-Ups – Easier variation of push-ups that targets the chest and shoulders with less strain.
    Do 3 sets of 10–12 reps.



  3. Push-Ups – A classic bodyweight move that works the chest, triceps, and shoulders.
    Do 3 sets of 8–12 reps.



  4. Wide Push-Ups – Focuses more on the chest and shoulders by widening your hand placement.
    Do 3 sets of 8–10 reps



  5. Tricep Dips – Strengthens and tones the triceps, giving arms better definition.
    Do 3 sets of 10–12 reps.



  6. Knee Push-Ups – Beginner-friendly push-up variation that builds strength in arms and chest.
    Do 3 sets of 10–15 reps.
