Back & Shoulder Workout

Warm-Up for back & shoulder Exercises

Before jumping into back & shoulder workouts, it’s important to warm up your body. A good warm-up increases blood flow, loosens your muscles, and gets your core ready to work harder while reducing the risk of injury. Start with 3–5 minutes of light cardio, like jogging in place or jumping jacks, to raise your heart rate. Then, add some dynamic stretches such as torso twists, standing side bends, or high knees to activate your core. This short routine will prepare your abs and make your workout more effective.

Chest Exercises

  1. Arm Circles – A great warm-up move that loosens shoulders and increases blood flow.
    Do 3 sets of 15–20 circles forward and backward.



  2. Cat-Cow Stretch – A gentle back stretch that improves flexibility and reduces stiffness.
    Do 3–4 rounds, 20 seconds each.



  3. Inchworms – A dynamic move that stretches the back and hamstrings while engaging the shoulders and arms for strength.
    Do 3 sets of 8–10 reps.



  4. Supine Push-Ups – This exercise strengthens the upper back, shoulders, and arms while improving posture and stability.
    Do 3 sets of 10–12 reps.



  5. Child’s Pose – A gentle stretch that relaxes the lower back, shoulders, and spine, relieving tension.
    Hold for 20–30 seconds, repeat 2–3 times.



  6. Hindu Push-Ups – A dynamic exercise that strengthens shoulders, chest, and upper back while improving flexibility and mobility.
    Do 3 sets of 8–12 reps.
